// Evolution of NLP
From Rules to Transformers

You'll understand each era of NLP ‚Äî not just the latest models. Knowing where each approach fails is what separates engineers who debug well from those who don't.

Pre-2013 ¬∑ Classical Era

Bag-of-Words & TF-IDF

Simple frequency-based representations. Still fast, interpretable, and surprisingly effective for short-text classification tasks with small datasets.

‚úì Taught in Week 1
2013‚Äì2017 ¬∑ Embedding Era

Word2Vec, GloVe & fastText

Dense vector representations that capture semantic relationships. The first time "king ‚àí man + woman ‚âà queen" worked mathematically. Introduced contextual meaning to NLP.

‚úì Taught in Week 2
2017‚Äì2018 ¬∑ Seq2Seq Era

LSTMs, GRUs & Attention

Recurrent models for sequential text. Attention mechanisms let models focus on relevant parts of input ‚Äî the crucial precursor to transformers.

‚úì Conceptual coverage in Week 3
2018 ¬∑ The Transformer Revolution

BERT ‚Äî Bidirectional Transformer

Pre-trained on masked language modelling. Fine-tunable on any downstream task with minimal data. Still the workhorse of production NLP in Indian companies.

‚úì Deep dive + fine-tuning in Week 3‚Äì4
2019‚Äì2023 ¬∑ Specialised Models

T5, BART, RoBERTa, DistilBERT

Task-specific and efficiency-optimised variants. T5 and BART for generation tasks (summarisation, translation). DistilBERT for faster inference with minimal accuracy loss.

‚úì Taught in Week 4‚Äì5
2023‚ÄìPresent ¬∑ Bridge to GenAI

Sentence Transformers & RAG Foundations

Sentence-level embeddings for semantic search and retrieval. The direct foundation for RAG pipelines covered in the Generative AI course.

‚úì Taught in Week 5 (bridge to GenAI course)
